# Epic 4 Retrospective: Manage Registered Folders and Baselines

## Part 1: Epic Review

### Epic Goal

Let users inspect registered-folder state, stop tracking a folder without touching its files, and intentionally replace a trusted baseline after a reliable scan.

### Completed Stories

- Story 4.1 - Display Registered Folder Metadata (`done`)
- Story 4.2 - Unregister a Folder (`done`)
- Story 4.3 - Refresh a Registered Folder Baseline (`done`)

Epic 4 is `done`: 3 of 3 stories completed and reviewed.

### Major Capabilities Delivered

- Story 4.1 established deterministic, strictly read-only registered-folder metadata display, including stable root/ID ordering, invariant UTC timestamps, `Never` for an absent verification timestamp, and stored baseline counts.
- Story 4.2 established conflict-aware catalog mutation for `unregister <folder>`, removing exactly one registration while preserving surviving entries, metadata, baselines, ordering, and target files.
- Story 4.3 reused that mutation pattern for `refresh <folder>`, replaced one baseline only after a complete reliable scan, and preserved registered-folder identity, unrelated catalog state, and target-file safety.
- Shared catalog validation now protects list, lookup, and mutation paths from malformed or ambiguous persisted state.
- Deterministic CLI output, typed V1 error mapping, and the Core/CLI boundary remained intact across all three commands.

### Important Design Decisions

- Treat persisted catalog data as untrusted and validate the complete catalog before display, lookup, target access, or mutation.
- Keep read-only formatting pure and console-free in Core; keep command dispatch, writers, and exit-code mapping in CLI.
- Perform catalog changes as immutable, index-based transformations so identity, survivor objects, and ordering are explicit and testable.
- Route participating writers through one `SaveIfUnchanged` protocol with filesystem coordination, durable same-directory temporary writes, a final pre-replacement version check, and cleanup on failure.
- Report success only after persistence succeeds; never retry, merge, or fall back to unconditional overwrite after a conflict.
- Allow refresh to read and hash target files, but reject incomplete scans and never create, delete, move, rename, or write target content.

### Review Findings That Improved the Implementation

- Story 4.1 review hardened shared catalog validation against control characters, negative file sizes, and missing/default registration timestamps.
- Story 4.2 review closed the replacement-boundary race, replaced session-local coordination with a filesystem sidecar lock, distinguished missing catalogs from inaccessible or directory-valued paths, and added survivor list/verify regression coverage.
- Story 4.3 review blocked filesystem-link aliases from bypassing catalog containment, mapped hash-provider cryptographic failures into the typed scan-error contract, and synchronized the canonical story mirror.
- The regression suite grew from 151 tests after Story 4.1 review to 186 after Story 4.2 and 213 after Story 4.3, with final Release build, focused tests, formatting, dependency, boundary, scope, safety, and diff checks passing.

### Previous Retrospective Follow-Through

Both Epic 3 action items were completed. Story 4.1 incorporated deterministic ordering, never-verified rendering, malformed-catalog handling, and read-only proof. Stories 4.2 and 4.3 then defined and reused the shared conflict-aware mutation contract while preserving identity and prior/latest state on failure.

### Lessons Learned

- Absence of side effects needs direct proof through raw-byte, target-tree, and injected-seam tests.
- A compare-before-save check is insufficient unless coordination and version validation continue through the final replacement boundary.
- Immutable transformations plus deep survivor assertions make catalog mutations easier to reason about and safer to extend.
- Reliable refresh means rejecting partial snapshots, withholding success until persistence, and testing the complete `refresh -> verify` workflow.
- Review gates worked well: each story reused a reviewed contract instead of pulling later scope forward.

### Risks Carried Forward

- Filesystem coordination protects participating FolderPrint writers but cannot guarantee behavior against every external editor or filesystem implementation.
- Cross-platform catalog-path and relative-separator policy remains deferred and should not change incidentally in Epic 5.
- Epic 5 duplicate grouping must remain deterministic and semantically compatible with verification findings without coupling `DuplicateFinder` to verification or catalog management.
- Unreadable findings currently carry paths rather than rich reasons; Epic 5 should preserve the established typed behavior unless an explicit model change is approved.
